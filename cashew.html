Cashew - The smart Wallpaper

This project was a part of my Human factors in Information Design course, Emerging Interfaces.

We were expected to develop a product kick starter at the end of the semester, using agile methodology and with the help of feedbacks provided by our professor and batch mates at the end each iteration. It gave me exposure to both the research and designing fields of HFID.

We were a team of 5 and came up with a smart home solution for double income families. Our idea was to help people manage their daily routine better and put them in charge of their lives and the smart devices. Smart home technology allows people to simplify their lives by using sophisticated devices. These sophisticated devices end up controlling their lives, though it is supposed to be vice-versa.

  • Our strategy was to Allow for an organic (not rigid) evolution of routines
  • Exceptions are really part of the routine
  • Easily establish new behaviors and modify existing behavior
  • Allow multiple, overlapping, and occasionally conflicting user goals
  • The smart home should know “who’s who”
  • Make interacting with the smart home optional
  • It’s only there when you need it

Wireframing tools used: Balsamiq and Axure.
